IMPROVEMENT IN NAVIGATIONAL CHANNELS




Just as in the Canal entrances, the widening and deepening of the navigation channels played a vital role in facilitating the passage of neopanamax vessels through the interoceanic waterway

However, unlike the activities conducted at the Atlantic and Pacific entrances, the dredging operations in Culebra Cut and Gatun Lake were executed by the Panama Canal Authority since its personnel possessed extensive experience in this field and utilized their own machinery, including the Mindi dredger, the Barú drilling barge, and the Quibián I cut and suction dredger.

Relevant facts:
The Panama Canal Authority only rented one piece of equipment for these tasks; the Il Principe dredger which was owned by the Belgian company Jan De Nul.

In total, an impressive 150 million m3 of material were excavated and dredged as part of the expansion project.
