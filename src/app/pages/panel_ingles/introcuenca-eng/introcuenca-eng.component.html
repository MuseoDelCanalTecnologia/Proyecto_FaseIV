A watershed is a land area that channels rainfall to streams and rivers that discharge in lakes, reservoirs and the sea.

The Canal watershed is synonymous with biodiversity due to its rich presence of flora and fauna. To preserve it, 3,453 km2 of land have been designated for the creation of six protected areas which account for 39% of the watershed’s surface area.

The Canal Watershed plays a vital role in the country because it supplies drinking water to more than 50% of the Panamanian population and allows the operation of the inter-oceanic waterway.
