THE PANAMA CANAL AND ITS CONNECTIONS




From its emergence to the present, this Isthmus has served as a vital crossroad for the movement of animals, people, ideas, and goods. With the inauguration of the Panama Canal in 1914, world maritime trade was revolutionized by significantly reducing travel distances and facilitating the transit of ships between the Pacific and Atlantic Oceans.

Currently, with the expansion of the interoceanic route and the transit of larger vessels, the Panama Canal connects about 1920 ports across 170 countries along 5 continents through a network of 180 maritime routes. This has positioned Panama as a pivotal global logistics hub that is complemented by its Pacific and Atlantic ports and the interoceanic railroad.
