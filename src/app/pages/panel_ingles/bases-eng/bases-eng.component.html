MILITARY BASES






For the United States, the Canal had a strategic value that transcended its economic importance. For this reason, the United States Government built a system of fortifications in the Atlantic and Pacific sectors to defend the interoceanic waterway and the continent.

However, with the implementation of the Torrijos-Carter Treaties and the end of the Cold War, these military bases gradually lost their relevance and were eventually reverted to Panama.
