IMPROVEMENTS TO THE WATER SUPPLY




This component was aimed to ensure a sustainable water supply for both Panama’s population and the Canal’s operational requirements, while also maximizing the utilization of existing water resources to avoid the necessity of constructing new reservoirs

To achieve these objectives, the improvements to the water supply included the construction of 18 water-saving basins, the deepening of Gatun Lake’s navigational channels, and the rising the maximum operating level of the lake from 26.7 m to 27.1 m.

Relevant facts:

The elevation of the maximum operating level of Gatun Lake resulted in the addition of approximately 200 million m³ to the Canal's water reserve.
