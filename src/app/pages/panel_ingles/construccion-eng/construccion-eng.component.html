This contract was awarded to the consortium known as Grupo Unidos por el Canal S.A. (GUPCSA), which was comprised of Sacyr Vallehermoso from Spain, Impregilo from Italy, Jan De Nul from Belgium, and Constructora Urbana from Panama. The original cost of the project amounted to 3.2 billion dollars.

Each lock complex features rolling gates for enhanced efficiency and nine water-saving basins designed to reuse up to 60% of the water required for each transit. The Neopanamax locks consume 7% less water per transit compared to the Panamax locks.
