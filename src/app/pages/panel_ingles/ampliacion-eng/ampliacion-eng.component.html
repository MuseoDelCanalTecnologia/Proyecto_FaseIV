Following the transfer of the Canal to Panamanian control on December 31, 1999, maintenance and modernization programs continued to enhance the competitiveness and efficiency of the interoceanic waterway.

Through the expansion project, the most significant endeavor undertaken by the Panamanian administration to date, the Canal's role in global maritime trade was greatly bolstered. Currently, this waterway connects approximately 1,920 ports in 170 countries via 180 maritime routes, solidifying Panama's status as a global logistics hub.
