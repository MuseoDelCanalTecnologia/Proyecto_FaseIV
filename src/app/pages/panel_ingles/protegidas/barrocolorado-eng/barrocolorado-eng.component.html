BARRO COLORADO NATURAL MONUMENT






Creation date: 1972, 1979
Area: 54km2
Location: Panama Oeste and Colon provinces
Information: Barro Colorado Island was formed in 1914 as a result of the flooding of Gatun Lake. In 1923, it was declared a permanent biological reserve, and in 1946, the Smithsonian Institution was entrusted with the island's administration for scientific studies.
Based on international agreements, Panama designated Barro Colorado Island as a natural monument in 1972, and with the signing of the Torrijos-Carter Treaties, the Smithsonian was recognized as the custodian of this protected area. Finally, in 1979, some adjacent peninsulas were added to expand the area for scientific research and the conservation of fauna and flora.
