FOREST COVER






Forests are a fundamental part of any watershed. Thanks to them, the soil can absorb water during the rainy season and release it during the dry season, reducing the effects caused by droughts.

Despite their importance, forest cover in the Canal watershed decreased from 80% to 20% during the second half of the 20th century due to deforestation.

Currently, as a result of reforestation programs and the creation of protected areas, these forests have begun to recover and now cover 61% of the watershed’s surface.
