COCO SOLO






Inauguration date: c. 1920s.
Original name: Coco Solo Naval Reservation
Previous use: : served as a base for submarines and, during the Second World War, it functioned as an aeronaval station. After this conflict, the military use of Coco Solo diminished and was destined for civilian purposes.

Reversion date: 1999.
Current name: Coco Solo
Current use: this area was divided into two sections and a container port was established in each one: Colon Container Terminal (CCT), in the north, and Manzanillo International Terminal (MIT) in the south.
