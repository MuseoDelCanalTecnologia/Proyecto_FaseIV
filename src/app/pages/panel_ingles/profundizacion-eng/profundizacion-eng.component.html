DEEPENING OF CANAL ENTRANCES




The deepening of the Canal’s Atlantic and Pacific entrances represented a pivotal component of the expansion project as it facilitated the passage of Neopanamax vessels through the interoceanic waterway.

The dredging of the Pacific sector was entrusted to the Belgian company Dredging International, while in the Atlantic sector another Belgian company named Jan de Nul was awarded the contract.

Relevant facts:

To accomplish these undertakings, both companies deployed remarkable dredging vessels, including the D'Artagnan for the Pacific and the De Bougainville for the Atlantic, among others.

In both the Pacific and Atlantic sectors, the entrance was expanded to a minimum width of 225 m and deepened to 15.5 m below mean low tide.
