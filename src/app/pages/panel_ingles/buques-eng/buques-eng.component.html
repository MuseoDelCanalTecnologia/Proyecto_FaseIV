Dry bulk carriers:

They transport dry grains such as corn, soy, wheat, and other bulk.
Crude product tankers :

They transport liquid bulk that include crude oil, petroleum, and petroleum-derived products (diesel, gasoline, jet fuel, etc.), chemicals and petroleum chemicals, among other products.
Gas vessels: :

This type of ship transports Liquified Petroleum Gas (LPG) and Liquified Natural Gas (LNG).
Refrigerated cargo ship::

They transport perishable products, including fruits, meats, and dairy products.
Passenger vessels:

Another part of the demand is the cruise industry, which sees in the region different tourist destinations.
Vehicle carriers:

They transport cars, trucks, and project cargo (goods that cannot be placed in a container due to their size). This category also includes Ro-Ros ships (Roll On-Roll Off ships equipped with ramps so that trucks can enter and exit to collect or leave cargo.)
Container carriers:

They transport all kinds of merchandise including furniture, clothing, household items, among others
OIL

Crude oil is transported from countries such as Ecuador and Colombia, passing through the Canal and then refined in the United States. Once this process has been carried out, it passes through the Panama Canal to countries such as Mexico, Ecuador, Peru, and Chile
Grains

The main grains that are transported are beans, soybeans, corn, and wheat. Most of the grains come from the United States and are destined to China, the South America’s west coast, Japan and the Central America’s west coast.
Gas
95% of the liquefied petroleum gas (LPG) that passes through the Canal comes from the United States, followed by Trinidad and Tobago with 3%, and is destined to South Korea and Japan. Approximately 5% of the world’s liquefied natural gas (LNG) passes through the Panama Canal.
Others

An example of a refrigerated product is the banana that is produced by Ecuador that passes through the Canal to the United States and Europe.
Containerized cargo

Products such as furniture, clothing and household items are transported in containers and they mostly originate from China, bound for Atlantic countries such as Argentina, Venezuela, Brazil, and the Caribbean.
Vehicles

Cars and electric appliances made in Japan and South Korea also cross the Canal bound for the Atlantic Ocean.
